Treatments for OCD

There are two proven treatment options for OCD: behaviour threapy and medication.

Behaviour Therapy

Behaviour therapy involves preventing the response, for example washing your hands or burshing your teeth, and graded exposure. That is, the more a compulsion is resisted, the easier it becomes to resist next time, however, the more you give in to a compulsion makes it harder to resist. For some, resisting compulsions might be easy, and for others it might be very difficult. Behaviour therapy can be daunting at first so seek help from a physcologist or a physicatrist if you need it. Medication

Treating OCD with medications involves the use of Serotonin Specific Re-uptake Inhibitors (SSRIs). For most who have tried using medication to treat their OCD, they find that their complusions are still there, but are less frequent and less severe. However, it should be noted that the effectiveness of using medication for OCD varys from person to person. Studies show 50% of people who used medication have seen an improvement in symptoms, compared to 70% for behaviour therapy.
