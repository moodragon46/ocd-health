Obsessive Compulsive Disorder

What is OCD?


OCD (obsessive compulsive disorder) is a type of anxiety disorder. This disorder causes recurring anxious thoughts that can become obsessive. A person with OCD will compulsively act to try and neutralise their obsessive thoughts.
OCD victims often feel shamed when they carry out compulsive actions as they feel they could be socially unaccepted. As they feel shamed, it can lead to the person not getting help as early as they should.


What are symptoms of OCD?

  • Obsessive need for cleanliness
  • Exaggerated fear of sickness or contamination
  • Needing for placements to be in patterns
  • Repeatedly counting items
  • Hoarding items
  • Obsessive fear of harm to oneself or to others
  • Continuously checking in case of forgetting items
  • If religious, feeling the need to pray countless times a day
  • Also having other disorders such as
    1. Anxiety
    2. Depression
    3. Eating disorders
    4. Other similar disorders
