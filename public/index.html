What is OCD?


OCD (obsessive compulsive disorder) is a type of anxiety disorder. This disorder causes recurring anxious thoughts that can become obsessive. These thoughts can be things such as, 'Are my hands clean?' or 'Did I lock the door?'. A person with OCD will compulsively act to try and neutralise their obsessive thoughts. For example, if a person with OCD is afraid that someone will break into their house, they will repeatedly check that their front door is locked. The fear of robbery and to double check the door is normal, but when it becomes obsessive (as in checking the door numerous times before leaving), it begins to become a problem that interupts day to day life.
In Australia, up to 3% of people will experience OCD in their lifetime, and up to 2% in a 12 month period. It can happen to anyone during any point in their lives, and even young children may show symptoms. However, in younger children it doesn't fully develop until they are older.
OCD victims often feel shamed when they carry out compulsive actions as they feel they could be socially unaccepted. As they feel shamed, it can lead to the person not getting help as early as they should.
